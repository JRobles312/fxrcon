Quick Tips

Things every homeowner should know.

💡 Did you know a hairline crack in your foundation can double in size within one rainy season?+
Water seeps into micro-cracks, freezes and expands, then widens the gap. Seal cracks under ¼ inch yourself with hydraulic cement. Anything wider needs a pro assessment — it could indicate settling or drainage issues.
💡 Did you know most bathroom remodels require a permit in California?+
Moving or adding plumbing, electrical, or structural walls always requires a permit — even in your own home. Skipping this can block a future sale or trigger a code enforcement case.
💡 Did you know your driveway slope affects water drainage and city compliance?+
California requires driveways to drain away from the structure. A slope of 1–2% is ideal. Too steep causes runoff issues; too flat causes pooling. FXR grades every pour to spec.
💡 Did you know an ADU can increase your property value by up to 30%?+
California's ADU laws have made it easier than ever to add a unit. Many cities in the Central Valley now have streamlined permit processes. FXR handles the full scope from permits to move-in ready.
💡 Did you know a sagging roofline usually means a compromised rafter or ridge board?+
This is a structural issue, not just cosmetic. If you see a dip in the roofline from the street, call a contractor before your next rainstorm. Left unaddressed it can lead to rafter failure.
Code Cases & Red Flags

Everything you need to know about code enforcement — and how to fix it fast.

RED FLAG
What Is a Code Enforcement Case — And What Happens Next?
Received a notice from the city? Here's exactly what it means, what your timeline looks like, and what NOT to do when you get that letter.
A code enforcement case is opened when a city inspector or neighbor complaint triggers a review of your property. Common triggers include unpermitted structures, tall grass, junk vehicles, or visible construction without a permit. Once a case is opened, you'll receive a Notice of Violation (NOV) with a correction deadline — typically 30 to 60 days. Missing this deadline escalates the case to fines, hearings, or in extreme cases, city-hired contractors at your expense. FXR specializes in responding to these cases quickly — we pull the permits, prepare the corrective work, and handle the inspection process so you don't have to navigate city bureaucracy alone.
WARNING
What Happens If You Build Without a Permit in California?
It might seem like a shortcut — but unpermitted work can cost you far more in the long run. Here's the real risk.
California requires permits for almost any structural, electrical, plumbing, or mechanical work. Building without one can result in: (1) Stop-Work Orders if an inspector visits during construction; (2) Red Tags requiring you to halt all activity; (3) Legalization costs that often exceed the original project; (4) Sale complications — buyers' agents and lenders flag unpermitted work, often killing deals. FXR has helped dozens of homeowners and investors navigate this process cleanly.
SOLUTION
How FXR Resolves Code Violations — Step by Step
We've resolved red tags, stop-work orders, and permit issues across the Central Valley. Here's exactly how our process works.
Step 1: Site Visit — We assess the property and identify what work is needed. Step 2: Permit Pull — We submit all required permit applications to the city. Step 3: Corrective Work — We perform all necessary construction to achieve compliance. Step 4: Final Inspection — We coordinate with the city inspector and are on-site for the walkthrough. Step 5: Case Closure — We confirm the case is closed and provide documentation. Most cases are resolved within 30–90 days.
INSIGHT
Red Tag vs. Stop-Work Order: What's the Difference?
Both are serious — but they're not the same thing. Know the difference so you can respond correctly.
A Red Tag (Notice of Non-Compliance) is placed on a structure by a building inspector, indicating the building is unsafe or not compliant. It prohibits occupancy. A Stop-Work Order is issued during active construction, halting all work until permits are pulled. A red tag is more serious — it can affect tenants and trigger relocation requirements. Both require immediate action. Call FXR at 408-769-9928 before responding to the city on your own.
